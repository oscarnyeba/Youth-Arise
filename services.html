Your Questions Answered

Do I need to be in crisis to reach out?

Not at all. We're here for everything from everyday stress to serious crises. You don't need to wait until things are unbearable‚Äîearly support often prevents bigger challenges down the road.

Are peer counselors really trained?

Yes. Every peer counselor completes 40+ hours of training covering active listening, mental health basics, crisis assessment, cultural sensitivity, and ethical boundaries. They receive ongoing supervision from licensed mental health professionals.

What if I need professional therapy?

Peer counselors can help with many challenges, but they're not a replacement for professional therapy. If you need clinical treatment, we'll connect you with affordable or free mental health professionals in your area.

Will you tell my parents or school?

Everything you share is confidential. The only exception is if we believe you or someone else is in immediate danger‚Äîin those rare cases, we involve professionals who can help keep everyone safe.

What happens in a peer counseling session?

You talk, your counselor listens. There's no judgment or advice-giving. Together, you'll explore what you're experiencing, identify your strengths, and consider your options. You're always in control of what you share.

Can I see the same counselor multiple times?

Absolutely. Many people build ongoing relationships with their peer counselors. You can request the same person for future sessions if you felt a good connection.

Ready to Take the First Step?

Whatever you're going through, you don't have to face it alone. Our community is here, ready to support you.
