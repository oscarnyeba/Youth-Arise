Our Story

Building a movement where every young person feels valued, heard, and supported in their mental health journey.

Why We Started

Youth Arise began in 2020 when a group of university students noticed something troubling: their friends were struggling with anxiety, depression, and stress, but few felt comfortable seeking help. The stigma around mental health created invisible barriers that left too many young people suffering in silence.

What started as informal peer support circles in campus study rooms has grown into a comprehensive mental health initiative serving thousands of students across Uganda. We realized that sometimes, the most powerful support comes not from professionals in white coats, but from peers who truly understand what you're going through.

Today, Youth Arise connects young people with trained peer counselors, evidence-based resources, and a community that breaks down the walls of isolation. We're building a generation that talks openly about mental health, supports one another without judgment, and knows where to turn when life gets overwhelming.

What Guides Us

The principles at the heart of everything we do

Peer-Driven Support

We believe young people are the experts on their own experiences. Our peer-to-peer model creates authentic connections that professional settings sometimes can't match. When someone who's been through similar struggles offers support, healing happens.

üîí

Confidentiality Always

Trust is everything. We maintain strict confidentiality in all our services, creating safe spaces where young people can share their struggles without fear of judgment, gossip, or unwanted disclosure to parents or schools.

Evidence-Based Approach

While our delivery is peer-to-peer, our methods are grounded in proven mental health practices. Every peer counselor receives professional training in active listening, crisis assessment, and when to refer to clinical professionals.

Accessible for All

Strength-Focused

We don't see young people as problems to be fixed. Every person has inherent strengths and resilience. Our role is to help individuals recognize and build on what's already working, even in their darkest moments.

Prevention Matters

While crisis support is crucial, we're equally committed to prevention. Our workshops teach coping skills, emotional regulation, and how to recognize warning signs in yourself and friends before things reach a breaking point.
